How To Be A Journalist

With great power comes great responsibility -- and that doesn't just apply to Spiderman. Thanks to the first amendment, which guarantees “freedom of the press,” journalists have special rights and protections when doing their jobs. But in order not to abuse that power, we follow a code of ethics that ensure the people we talk to, photograph, or film also have rights. And as a reporter for Youth Radio, we take additional steps to ensure we are representing our sources with accuracy and dignity.


Step 1:

The foundation of journalism is asking people to talk to you. Your source will need you to be clear about what you're asking them to do in order to consent to the story. So as you prepare to email, text, or pick up the phone to ask for an interview, you'll want to follow a few best practices:

YR DO-IT CHECKLIST - CONTACTING SOURCES
  • DO Identify yourself as a journalist/reporter
  • DO make it clear you're working for Youth Radio
  • DO state the story may be seen by a national audience
  • DO briefly state what the story is about
  • DO make it clear what you want (interview or background-only)
  • DO make it clear what the type of media (video, audio, photo, text)
  • DO state your deadline
  • DO give your contact info
  • DO follow up via phone, email, text, etc. if you don't hear back!
YR DON'T CHECKLIST - CONTACTING SOURCES
  • DON'T make it sound like you're doing a project for school or working for a school paper
  • DON'T promise anonymity or an off-the-record interview
  • DON'T misrepresent yourself or the what the story is about
  • DON'T apologize for bothering someone
  • DON'T promise someone they will be a part of a story (they may get cut in edit)
  • DON'T promise someone an outlet when you're not sure where a story is going
  • DON'T try to be both a reporter AND an advocate or friend at the same time

Try it yourself!

ID SELF

Hi! I'm Asha. Mind if I talk to you about immigration?

Hi! I'm Asha. I write a column for the school paper and I'm reporting a story about immigration.

Hi! I'm Asha. I'm in your history class. I'm reporting a story for Youth Radio about immigration.

WHAT'S THE STORY?

I'm getting people to talk about how teens think Trump's immigration plan is stupid.

I'm doing a radio story about how young people feel about immigration reform and how here in San Diego we probably feel differently than other parts of the country and I want to find people who agree and disagree and highlight the tension of what it means for us all to be going to school in the same place.

I'm doing a radio story about how young people feel about immigration policy.

WHY THIS SOURCE?

I really liked that article in the school paper you wrote about how immigration policy affects your family. I think you have important things to say on this topic.

I'm not sure if you're the right person to ask about this, but I figured, why not?

I'm looking to talk to any student at our school about this.

THE ASK

Mind if I ask you a few questions about how you feel about this?

Could I do a short audio interview with you about your experience? I think it will take about 15 minutes.

I'm really really sorry to bother you about this, but I need someone to say they are anti-immigration. Can you say that for me? I know, this whole thing is stupid but my editor wants me to do it.

WHO IS THE AUDIENCE?

This story may air nationally in addition to appearing on Youth Radio's site.

Just to be clear, this story is for Youth Radio. It's a blog.

Don't be nervous about sharing. No one from school will ever see this.

DEADLINE

I totally get that you're busy. If you're free sometime soon, that would be cool.

No hurry to get back to me.

I have until next Monday to gather interviews for the story. Are you available to talk this week?

THE STAKES

I know this story is sensitive, so let me know if you don't want to use your name.

I think this is a very timely topic, and your perspective would add a lot to the conversation.

This topic is totally embarrassing to talk about, so if I were you I'd just make up a name. No one will know the difference.

FOLLOW UP

If you're interested, is there any chance you're free to talk today or tomorrow? i'm free at lunch or after school. Thank you!

Let me know if you're interested whenever it's convenient. Thank you!
